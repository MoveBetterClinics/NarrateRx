Interview transcript

What you actually said — compare the drafts against the source. Phrases used verbatim in a draft are highlighted.

Q: So when someone comes in with neck pain, what’s the first thing you tell them?

Dr. Q: Honestly? Most “neck pain” isn’t really about the neck. It’s how your mid-back and shoulders stopped sharing the load. People sit all day, the upper back locks up, and the neck ends up doing all the work.

Q: So what do you actually do about it?

Dr. Q: There’s a 30-second reset I give every desk-bound patient. You open the chest, get the mid-back moving again, and the neck finally gets a break. I tell them to try it at night and notice how the morning feels.

Q: And that holds up?

Dr. Q: When we restore that sharing of the load, yeah — the neck stops flaring up. That’s the whole point.
