How it actually works

01

Talk for 15 minutes.

NarrateRx prompts you with the right questions about what you actually do — your method, the thing you saw this week that lit you up, the question people keep asking you. You answer out loud. That's the work.

02

NarrateRx drafts the month.

One conversation becomes an email newsletter, a batch of social posts, Google Business Profile copy, and the supporting media — exported clean and ready to drop into the tools you already use. All in your voice. All from your ideas.

03

Review, tweak, paste.

You skim, edit anything that needs it, paste it where it goes — Mailchimp, your scheduler, your VA's queue, wherever you already publish. Thirty minutes a week instead of eight hours. Or $800 a month for content you don't recognize.

Real content. Real person.

Every other tool in this space tries to fake your voice. They scrape your website, ingest a few posts, and have an LLM impersonate you. The output is fluent and forgettable, because there's nothing actually in it.

NarrateRx works the other way around. The interview is the product. Your voice and your ideas are the input. The AI is the assembler — it organizes what you already said into formats that fit each channel. It doesn't invent your expertise. It captures it.

That's why the output sounds like you. Because it is you.

FAQ

What does "founding owner" mean in practice?
Locked-in early pricing, hands-on onboarding from me, and direct input on the roadmap. Capped at ten owners.
How long does the interview actually take?
Fifteen minutes is the target. Some owners go thirty because they get on a roll. Either way, it's once a month.
What do I actually get out of each interview?
An email-ready newsletter, a month of social copy for Instagram and Facebook, Google Business Profile posts, and the supporting media — all formatted clean and ready to paste into whatever tools you already use. You stay in control of where it goes.
Won't it still sound like AI?
No, and that's the whole point. The output is built from your words, not generated from a prompt. If a draft ever sounds generic, that's a bug — tell me and I'll fix it.
Who is this not for?
Owners without a clear point of view. If your marketing today is "best service, great prices, friendly staff," NarrateRx won't help. It amplifies what's already there. It can't manufacture it.
