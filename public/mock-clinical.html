What clinical owners ask first

Calibrated for the way you actually work.

NarrateRx isn't a generic content tool with healthcare paint. The pipeline is built around the constraints of running a practice.

Built for between-patient minutes

The interview is short on purpose. Fifteen minutes recorded, fifteen minutes to review the drafts. Total monthly time budget: 30 minutes.

De-identified by design

Prompts steer toward de-identified clinical narrative. Review queue is the final gate before anything goes out. No recordings retained past session.

Patient-language vocabulary

Voice rules tuned per vertical — chiropractic, equine, animal chiro, coaching. The model knows your audience reads "your dog who can't get up the stairs," not "mobility-impaired companion animal."

Four channels from one interview

Blog post, social batch, newsletter, Google Business update — drafted in parallel from the same transcript. One conversation becomes a month of channels.

Review queue is the brain

Approve, reject, or schedule each draft. The model learns which phrases you accept and rejects, and tunes next month's drafts to need less surgery.

Your stack, your tools

Buffer for social. WordPress or Astro for the blog. Clean exports for TrustDrivenCare, Mailchimp, or anything else. We don't trap your content.
