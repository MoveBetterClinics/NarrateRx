How it works, step by step

From talking for 15 minutes to a month of posts.

Most "AI for content" tools hand you a blank box and wait for you to type. NarrateRx starts with you talking. Here's exactly what happens between the moment you start the conversation and the moment a post goes live on your Google listing — in plain English.

The five steps

Five steps. You only do the first one.

01

You answer a few questions out loud

Four to six questions, picked for your kind of work and your audience. Talk into the app — on your phone, in the car between patients. The questions are designed to pull out the real story and the strong opinion you'd share with a patient, not the stiff brochure version.

02

It writes down exactly what you said

Every word is captured, word for word — nothing summarized or smoothed away. If a name gets misheard, you fix it in one place and every draft updates. What you actually said stays the foundation for everything that follows.

03

It finds the best ideas

NarrateRx reads back through what you said and pulls out the strongest ideas — the patient story, the belief, the question you get asked over and over. Each one becomes the seed for a piece of content.

04

It writes a draft for each place you post

The same idea is reshaped to fit each spot: a blog post, social captions, a newsletter, a Google update. Links and hashtags handled for you. The substance stays yours — NarrateRx just does the formatting.

05

You review and publish

Skim, tweak, approve. One click posts to your social and Google listing; clean copies for anything else you use. NarrateRx remembers what you change and needs fewer fixes every month.

Step 1 · The conversation

The questions are the secret.

A bad question gets you a bland answer. NarrateRx asks the questions that pull the case, the conviction, the unguarded line — the stuff your audience never sees on your website.

Questions an agency asks

  • "What services do you offer?"
  • "What sets you apart from competitors?"
  • "Who is your target customer?"
  • "What are your top three benefits?"

→ Answers sound like a brochure. Because they are.

Questions NarrateRx asks

  • "What did you see this week that stuck with you?"
  • "What question are patients asking you over and over right now?"
  • "What's a belief you hold about your work that most peers don't?"
  • "What's the last thing you said that made someone go quiet?"

→ Answers sound like you. Because they're yours.

Step 2 · Getting it down

Every word — exactly as you said it.

The same kind of speech technology behind professional medical scribes writes down exactly what you said. Nothing is summarized or paraphrased. That record of your own words is what every post is built from.

Want to tidy something up first? You can edit it, or fix a misheard name in one place and watch every draft update. Your words stay the foundation; everything built on top is yours to change.

Steps 3 & 4 · Finding ideas + writing

The same idea, reshaped for each place you post.

AI is great at reshaping words and terrible at inventing a point of view. So NarrateRx only does the part it's good at: it takes a real idea you already had and writes it up for each place it needs to go. Same idea, several shapes.

Long-form blog post

800–1,200 word post with hook, three-act structure, internal links pulled from your real site map, and a CTA that points to your booking flow.

Social captions × 6

Two text-first posts. Two short-form scripts. Two carousels. Each with a hook variant tested to perform on the platform it's going to.

Email newsletter

Subject line, preview text, hero quote, three body paragraphs, PS line. Drops straight into your TrustDrivenCare or Mailchimp template.

Google Business post

Short updates sized to fit Google's limits, with your town in the text and a link to the new blog post. Posted for you, or copied out clean.

Hero image + thumbnails

AI-generated cover art tuned to your brand palette, plus 1080×1080 social variants. Stop paying for stock photos that look like everyone else's.

Clean exports

Plain HTML, Markdown, or copy-paste-safe rich text for every output. No "open in our app to read this" friction. Your stack, your tools.

Step 5 · Review & publish

Nothing goes out until you say so.

Your drafts land in one simple list. Skim them, pick which ones go out this month, edit a headline or quote right there, and drag them around the calendar.

Three ways to publish

  • Post it for you — social and your Google listing go live on your schedule.
  • Send it to your website — blog posts publish straight to your real site.
  • Copy it out clean — paste into your email tool or anything else. Your content is never locked in.

It learns from your edits

Approve, skip, or schedule each piece. NarrateRx remembers the words you cut and the headlines you rewrite — and writes the next month closer to how you'd say it, so there's less to fix each time.
