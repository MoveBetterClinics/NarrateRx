The architecture

Six things no one else does at once.

Pieces of NarrateRx exist in other tools — the transcription, the LLM, the publishing. The leverage is in the combination, and in one architectural decision the rest of the market hasn't made.

01

Interview-first architecture

The only category that starts with your voice in audio form. Castmagic starts with a podcast. Jasper starts with a prompt. We start with a 15-minute conversation the model can build a month around.

02

Four channels from one interview

Blog post, six social variants, newsletter, and Google Business update — drafted in parallel from the same transcript. Every other tool requires four interviews and four briefs.

03

Voice fingerprint that learns

Every accept and reject in the review queue tunes the next month's drafts. Generic AI tools have no memory of what you cut. We remember, and get quieter with the corrections each month.

04

Vertical-specific prompts

Chiro, equine, animal chiro, and coaching each get their own audience description, link library, and tone vocabulary. A horizontal AI writer has no opinion on whether "fur baby" is acceptable. We have a strong one.

05

Native publish, not just export

Buffer for social and Google Business Profile. WordPress and Astro for the blog itself. Clean exports for Mailchimp, TrustDrivenCare, and anything else. One click, not a copy-paste tour.

06

Built by an operator

Every workflow decision pressure-tested in a real three-clinic practice. We optimize for "the owner has thirty minutes a week between patients." Tools built by SaaS-natives optimize for marketers who have all day.
