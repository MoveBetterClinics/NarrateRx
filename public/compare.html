01

Interview-first architecture

We are the only category that starts with you talking. Castmagic starts with a podcast. Jasper starts with a prompt. We start with a 15-minute prompted Q&A that the rest of the pipeline is built around.

02

Four channels from one source

The same interview becomes a long-form blog post, six social variants, a newsletter, and a Google Business update — all in parallel. Every single-channel tool requires four interviews and four briefs.

Voice tuning loop

Every accept and reject in the review queue tunes the next month's draft. Generic AI tools have no memory of what you cut. NarrateRx remembers, and gets quieter with the corrections every month.

Where another tool might be the better pick.

We'd rather you pick the right tool than a bad fit. Here's when something else is probably better.

  • You want zero involvement. If you want fully automated, set-and-forget content that doesn't require you to talk for 15 minutes a month, a social VA or a templated automation tool is genuinely better. NarrateRx only works if you do the interview.
  • You're a podcast first. If your primary channel is a long-form audio show and you want clips and show notes, Castmagic or Descript is built for that. NarrateRx is built around a short, prompted interview — not long-form audio repurposing.
  • You need copywriting, not content. If you need high-conversion sales pages, landing page copy, or ad creative, a dedicated copywriting tool (or a good freelancer) is more appropriate. That's not the job we're optimizing for.
  • You need volume over voice. If you need 50 pieces of generic content a month and don't care whether it sounds like you, any $20/mo AI writer will do that faster and cheaper. We're not the volume play.
